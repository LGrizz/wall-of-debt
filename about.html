How student debt affects all Canadians

Debt isn’t just a problem for students; it’s a problem for families, communities, and the economy

The Biggest Barrier

Costs are the number one barrier to post-secondary education. More efficient financial assistance would help overcome this difficulty.

A Challenge for Families

More and more families are feeling the pinch of a restrictive financial aid model. In 2011, 42.3% of 20-29 years olds lived in their parental home compared to 26.9% in 1981.

An Economic Hurdle

Graduates with saddled with debt are less likely to own a home or vehicle, have children, get married, or have personal savings. Student debt is impacting Canada’s economy.

  • “42, 876 applicants were denied a loan or grant from the Canada Student Loans Program in 2011-2012.”

    - Canadian Alliance of Student Associations

  • “The sad reality is that most Canadians, and particularly those from low-income households, overestimate the cost and underestimate the benefit from post-secondary education.”

    - TD Economics

  • “Last year nearly one in three borrowers had financial needs that exceeded what their financial assistance would support.”

    - Canadian Alliance of Student Associations
